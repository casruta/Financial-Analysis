### CEO Background — Rich Kruger

- Appointed after activist pressure from Elliott Management following CEO Mark Little's resignation (2022)
- Over 40 years of global energy industry experience including extensive Canadian oil sands
- Chairman, President & CEO of Imperial Oil Limited (2013-2019)
- VP of Exxon Mobil Corporation and President of ExxonMobil Production Company (global upstream)
- Career at Exxon Mobil and predecessors since 1981
- Under his leadership at Suncor: record production, 100% refinery utilization, significant debt reduction, dramatically improved safety
- Base salary: $1,350,000 (unchanged from 2024)
- Did not receive pension; instead receives $2.3M in DSUs annually in lieu of pension/savings plan

## Governance Notes

### Elliott Management Activist Campaign (2022-2023)

- Elliott Investment Management took a significant stake in Suncor (~$3.4B reported)
- Pushed for operational improvements, safety overhaul, and leadership changes after a series of safety incidents
- CEO Mark Little resigned in July 2022 amid operational and safety concerns
- Kris Smith served as interim CEO (Jul 2022 - Apr 2023)
- Rich Kruger appointed President & CEO in April 2023, widely seen as Elliott-influenced selection
- Elliott secured board representation: several new directors joined July 2022 (Ashby, Seasons, Sheppard)
- Post-Kruger appointment results: record production, dramatically improved safety, accelerated debt reduction, $8B net debt target achieved ahead of schedule
- Elliott's campaign is widely cited as a successful catalyst for operational turnaround

### Key Governance Features

- **Voting:** Annual election of all directors; individual (not slate) director elections
- **Share ownership requirements:** Directors: substantial ownership requirement; CEO: 6x base salary; EVPs: 3x; SVPs: 2x
- **Say-on-pay:** Annual advisory vote; 2025 AGM: board nominees elected with strong support (Girling 97.06% approval)
- **Clawback policy:** In place for executive incentive compensation
- **No stock options** granted to non-employee directors
- **Director retirement policy** in place
- **Written business conduct code**
- **100% of director nominees with less than 10 years of tenure**
- **Dual listing:** TSX (SU) and NYSE (SU); compliant with applicable NYSE Corporate Governance Standards with Canadian variations noted
- **Annual Board, Chair, committee and director evaluations**
- **Board orientation and education program**
- **Annual principal risk review**
- **Advance notice by-law** in place
- **Independent auditor:** KPMG LLP (since March 2019); 2025 audit fees: $7.3M
- **Transfer agent:** Computershare Trust Company of Canada / Computershare Trust Company N.A.
